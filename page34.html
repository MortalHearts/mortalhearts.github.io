Sohmer & Co 

Sohmer & Co. was founded by German immigrant Hugo Sohmer and Austria-born Joseph Kuder, a former Steinway & Sons employee. They built their factory in Astoria, Queens in 1886 before the widespread availability of electricity, with an L-shaped plan and large windows to draw in sunlight. Demand for pianos was growing, and by 1925 they employed 275 people and produced 2,500 pianos a year. While they manufactured many models of piano, they specialized in uprights, like the one used for Surround Sounds Piano_QNS, which were known for having a more powerful sound than those of competing manufacturers. The factory closed in 1982 when the grandson of Hugo sold the company. The building has since been converted to residential use.
